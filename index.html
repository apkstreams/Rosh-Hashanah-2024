Rosh Hashanah 5785

Rosh Hashanah 2024 for Hebrew Year 5785 begins at sundown on Wednesday, 2 October 2024 and ends at nightfall on Friday, 4 October 2024.

Rosh Hashanah, also known as the Jewish New Year, is a time of reflection, repentance, and renewal for Jewish people around the world. It marks the beginning of the High Holy Days, a ten-day period of introspection culminating in Yom Kippur, the Day of Atonement.

During Rosh Hashanah, Jews gather in synagogues to pray, recite special prayers, and blow the shofar, a ram's horn, as a call to repentance. Traditional foods such as apples dipped in honey are eaten to symbolize hopes for a sweet new year.

It is also a time for personal reflection and introspection, as Jews examine their actions over the past year and seek forgiveness from others and from God. The holiday is a time for family gatherings, festive meals, and the sharing of blessings and good wishes for the coming year.

As the sun sets on Rosh Hashanah, Jews around the world wish each other "Shanah Tovah," a good and sweet new year, and look forward to the opportunities for growth, renewal, and blessings that the new year brings.
